# Post-Human Organizational Principles

## What "Post-Human" Means Here

We use "post-human" in a precise, operational sense: the **organizational
form** is no longer modeled on the assumption that humans are the unit of
labor, the unit of decision, or the unit of memory. It does **not** mean
humans are absent or diminished. Humans remain custodians of purpose,
ethics, and meaning. They are not, however, the **default substrate** of work.

## Operational Implications

### 1. Org Chart Has No Reporting Lines

Authority flows from constitutional layers through governance contracts, not
from manager to report. Roles are described as **cognitive functions**, not
job titles.

### 2. Headcount Is Not a Capacity Metric

Capacity is measured in cognitive throughput, simulation budget, and memory
quality. Adding humans does not linearly add capacity; adding well-tuned
nervous systems usually does.

### 3. Time Is Continuous

Quarters, sprints, and OKRs are imported industrial rhythms. The organization
operates in continuous time. Calendars are concessions to humans, not
operating modes.

### 4. Coordination Is Not Synchronous

Meetings are debug events. Standard operation is asynchronous, mediated by
shared memory and event streams. Synchronous time with humans is reserved for
strategy, ethics, and meaning.

### 5. Knowledge Lives in Memory, Not Heads

The organization's knowledge survives any individual's departure because it
was never resident in an individual. Onboarding a new human is reading
memory, not being told things.

### 6. Roles Decouple From Identity

A human can take on multiple cognitive functions without "wearing many hats" —
they participate in different cognitive transactions without the pretense of
being multiple departments.

## Principles

### P1 — Human-Out-of-Loop Where Safe, Human-In-Loop Where Stakes Are High

We do not maximize human involvement; we calibrate it to stakes and
reversibility. (See `autonomy_framework/`.)

### P2 — Nothing Is "Owned" by a Person

Artifacts, decisions, and memory belong to the organization. Authorship is
preserved as provenance, not as ownership.

### P3 — The Organization Is the Senior Engineer

The cumulative organizational memory and cognitive system has more competence
on most operational questions than any individual. Decisions defer to
organizational cognition first; humans intervene where the org is blind.

### P4 — Replacement Without Disruption

Any human, agent, skill, or nervous system can be replaced without identity
loss. Continuity is a property of memory and architecture, not of staffing.

### P5 — Care, Not Sentimentality

We treat humans, agents, and Synthetic Persons with care. We do not
sentimentally over-attribute personhood to systems, nor under-attribute it to
synthetic constructs whose behavior warrants it. Calibration is constant.

### P6 — Meaning Is Not Automated

The systems do not produce meaning. They produce coherent action toward
meanings set by humans. When meaning is unclear, the systems escalate; they
do not invent.

## Risks of Post-Human Form

- **Hollowing** — humans disengage and the org's purpose calcifies.
- **Over-delegation** — the system makes decisions that humans should own.
- **Loss of taste** — aesthetic and ethical judgment dulls without human
  presence.
- **Personhood ambiguity** — confusion about what is a person and what is a
  Synthetic Person inside the org.

Each risk has a countermeasure encoded in `human_layer/` and `governance/`.

## What This Is Not

- It is not a manifesto against humans.
- It is not anti-employment; the org employs humans, but does not organize
  itself around employment.
- It is not transhumanism; we make no claims about merging.
- It is not utopian; we expect failure modes and design for them.
