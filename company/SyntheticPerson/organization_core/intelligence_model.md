# Intelligence Model

## Definition

Organizational intelligence at SyntheticPerson.ai is the **measurable capacity
of the organism to produce coherent, context-grounded, simulation-validated
action over time, under uncertainty**.

Note what this excludes: raw model capability, agent count, content output,
hours worked. Those are inputs, not intelligence.

## Components

| Component | What it measures | How it is sensed |
|---|---|---|
| **Perceptual breadth** | Range of signals subsystems ingest | Stream coverage |
| **Memory depth** | Time horizon and density of recall | Retrieval evals |
| **Reasoning quality** | Soundness of inferences | Replay + ground truth |
| **Simulation fidelity** | How well simulations match outcomes | Backtests |
| **Coordination efficiency** | Cost of coherent multi-agent action | Event/decision ratio |
| **Adaptation rate** | Speed of useful internal change | Skill/agent evolution rate |
| **Coherence** | Internal consistency across subsystems | Contradiction frequency |

These compose into the **Organizational Intelligence Index (OII)**, tracked
continuously by the `learning_system`.

## Intelligence Levels

We track intelligence at five scales simultaneously:

1. **Skill** — does the primitive produce reliable outputs?
2. **Agent** — does the agent act competently in its role?
3. **Cluster** — does the cluster coordinate without thrash?
4. **Subsystem** — does the nervous system sense, reason, and act coherently?
5. **Organization** — does the whole behave like one mind?

A high score at one level does not guarantee a high score at others.

## Intelligence ≠ Volume

We refuse common proxies:

- We do not measure intelligence by output volume.
- We do not measure it by speed.
- We do not measure it by number of agents or models used.
- We do not measure it by tokens consumed.

These can correlate negatively with intelligence when memory is poor or
coordination is weak.

## Sources of Intelligence

1. **Memory** — the most important source; without memory, no intelligence
   compounds.
2. **Simulation** — second-order intelligence emerges from running
   counterfactuals.
3. **Coordination** — multiplier on the others.
4. **Models** — substrate, not source. Models change; intelligence persists in
   memory and architecture.

## How Intelligence Grows

- **Compounding memory** — every transaction adds context retrievable later.
- **Skill emergence** — recurring transactions become primitives.
- **Cluster maturation** — clusters develop internal protocols.
- **Subsystem specialization** — nervous systems deepen their domains.
- **Architectural evolution** — periodic restructuring guided by the learning
  system.

## How Intelligence Decays

- **Memory rot** — stale, contradictory, or poisoned artifacts.
- **Skill drift** — primitives that no longer match the world.
- **Coordination calcification** — over-codified protocols that block
  adaptation.
- **Human disengagement** — strategy without taste.
- **Model regression** — substrate degradation we do not detect.

The learning system continuously reads decay indicators.

## Intelligence Floor and Ceiling

The **floor** is set by memory quality and governance: even in a bad week, we
do not drop below it.

The **ceiling** is set by human-set purpose and ethical principles: we do not
chase intelligence past what our values allow.

## Trade-offs

- More aggressive memory growth raises ceiling, raises rot risk.
- More aggressive simulation budget raises fidelity, raises compute cost.
- More aggressive autonomy raises throughput, raises governance load.

Calibration is the work of the learning system, ratified by humans.
