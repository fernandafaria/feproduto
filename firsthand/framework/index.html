The AI-Native Maturity Framework

Six levels of organizational AI autonomy.

A unified framework merging Comp.vc's AI Maturity Map with Ann Miura-Ko's autonomy levels. Define where you are, what AI can see and do, who can extend the system, and how your organization has changed.

SOURCES

Built from two complementary frameworks.

Comp.vc AI Maturity Map — Five levels (N1–N5) defining the qualitatively distinct roles AI plays in an organization. Core principles: work backwards, think by area, close the loop from Day 0.

Ann Miura-Ko's Autonomy Levels — Six levels (L0–L5) based on four diagnostic questions: What can AI see? What can AI do? Who can extend the system? How has the organization changed? Field-tested across startups and scaled companies like Ramp.

L0AI as TheaterSpeeches ≠ adoption
See
Nothing structured. Knowledge lives in people's heads, undocumented meetings, unreadable SaaS tools.
Do
Nothing of consequence. Maybe summarize if a human pastes a transcript.
Extend
No one. AI is a personal, ungoverned tool.
Org Change
None. Same meetings, same processes.
Hard test: Can AI complete any recurring business process end-to-end?
False positive: CEO speeches about AI while running the same meeting structures.
L1Personal Productivity"80% use AI weekly" — meaningless
See
Only what each individual feeds it. Private prompts. No organizational visibility.
Do
Draft, summarize, brainstorm, code. No action on systems of record.
Extend
Each user reinvents. Power-user workflows leave with the person.
Org Change
None. Maybe a "Head of AI" hire buys some tools.
Hard test: If your best AI user left tomorrow, would their workflow remain in the company?
False positive: "80% of employees use AI weekly!" — probably true, completely meaningless.
L2Team WorkflowAI-enhanced silos ≠ AI-native org
See
Shared context per team (claude.md, shared prompts, MCP integrations). AI sees within team boundaries.
Do
Functional workflows: sales prospecting, support triage, code review. Bounded within a team's domain.
Extend
Non-engineers use shared workflows within the team. Across teams, not really. Each function builds private stacks.
Org Change
Functional efficiency gains. Hiring slows, org shape unchanged. Role boundaries intact.
Hard test: Does this workflow cross team boundaries, or is every function building its own private AI stack?
False positive: "We have AI workflows in every department." Collection of AI-enhanced silos ≠ AI-native organization.
L3Organizational InfrastructureCapture ≠ legibility
See
Whole org queryable. Cross-functional context accessible. Core systems of record exposed via APIs for agents to act.
Do
Agents act across systems: update CRMs, open PRs, route tickets, reconcile invoices. Cross-functional but bounded.
Extend
Non-engineers author shared skills. Sales packages call analysis. CX packages ticket investigation. Skills move horizontally.
Org Change
Org chart materially different from 2023. Zero-PM teams. PM-as-agent-orchestrator. Role convergence into "builders." Token-maxing over headcount-maxing.
Hard test: Can an agent answer, across systems: what shipped last sprint, who asked for it, what broke after launch, what customers said, and what the company should do next — without convening a cross-functional meeting?
False positive: Landfill of meeting transcripts and dashboards with no synthesis. An inert archive is not an operating system.
L4Compounding Operating SystemClosed loops scale quality
See
Relationships between events, not just events. System maintains context. Agents update agents. Skills marketplace propagates wins. System learns what to surface.
Do
Agents have policy-driven decision authority in scoped domains. Active removal of software blockers. Custom internal harnesses for core work.
Extend
Non-engineers ship production internal tools. Finance builds contract reviewer. AE ships sales tool in under an hour. Engineering only for production review.
Org Change
Hierarchy collapses toward "channel managers" of agent workflows. New archetype: agent-enabled builder. Explicit structural change — not just speed, shape.
Hard test: Does the system get smarter with every decision, or does quality require constant manual intervention?
False positive: "Human-in-the-loop" that's actually just governance (N3 with an extra review step), not learning (N4 with a closed loop).
L5Autonomous OrganizationNot yet empirically observed
See
Complete organizational awareness. System discovers its own blind spots. Autonomous learning from business outcomes.
Do
AI makes and executes decisions autonomously. Results improve month after month without human intervention. Decision quality surpasses best human judgment.
Extend
System self-extends. Agents create agents. Skills are generated, tested, and deployed without human authorship.
Org Change
Organization as platform. Humans set values and constraints. AI operates within them. Role of humans: define what "good" means.
Hard test: Does the company improve without any specific human intervention?
This level has not been empirically observed at scale. Frameworks are speculative.
THE DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS

Four questions to place any organization.

From Ann Miura-Ko. Answer these honestly — not aspirationally — to locate your company on the framework.

1. What can AI see?

The legibility of your company's work. Is it structured and queryable, or locked in people's heads and unreadable SaaS tools? Capture is not legibility. An inert archive is not an operating system.

2. What can AI do?

The ability to act on systems of record — not just observe, not just draft. Can agents update CRMs, open PRs, reconcile invoices? Summarizing meetings is not acting on systems.

3. Who can extend the system?

Broader authorship, not just engineers. Can a sales rep package call analysis? Can finance build a contract reviewer? If only engineers can extend, the system doesn't scale.

4. How has the organization changed?

Not just speed — shape. Has the org chart changed materially from 2023? Have roles converged? Are you token-maxing over headcount-maxing? Same org, same roles, same rituals, just faster = you're still at L1/L2.

THE PRINCIPLES

How to use the framework.

Work backwards, not stepwise.

The building blocks that sustain L4 do not emerge from optimizing L1 or L2. They must be designed from Day 0 for the target level. Ask: "What is the target level for this area, and which building blocks should be assembled now?"

Think by area, not by the whole organization.

Different areas (Engineering, Product, Sales, HR, Finance) mature at different paces. Treat each as an independent track with its own target level, timeline, and priorities.

Close the loop from Day 0.

Most organizations operate in open loops: agents produce outputs, nothing returns. Quality improvements require manual intervention. The element that separates the largest advances: the system's output produces an observable signal that returns and modifies future behavior. Open loops scale execution. Closed loops scale quality.
