THE AI MATURITY MAP

AI-native in 5 levels.

Each level represents a qualitatively different role for AI in how an area operates. Applied per area — not to the whole organization. Different areas coexist at different levels.

N1

AI as Individual Productivity

People use AI to optimize personal workflows. Some people execute the same processes with much higher efficiency.

Treating token usage as a success metric. "More tokens = more AI-native."
N2

AI as Team Productivity

Shared agentic tools used in human-designed workflows. An AI solution exists for nearly every operational task.

Counting agents and skills built. "More agents = more AI-native."
N3

AI as Contextualized Operating System

A single agentic layer executes any work within human-defined criteria. One person can operationalize an entire area's work.

Treating all data sources the same. "Connecting more sources = more accurate."
N4

AI as Decision Intelligence

An agentic layer makes decisions based on human learning and executes them. Decision quality converges to the level of the best human judgment.

Giving equal weight to all human feedback. "More feedback = better."
N5

AI as Adaptive Intelligence

An agentic layer makes the best decisions via autonomous learning. Area results improve month after month without human intervention.

N5 has not yet been empirically observed at scale.
PRINCIPLES

How we think about maturity.

Work backwards, not stepwise.

The building blocks that sustain N4 do not emerge from optimizing N1 or N2. They must be designed from Day 0 for the target level. Organizations that build agents piecemeal often face massive rework. The right question: "What is the target level for this area, and which of that level's building blocks should be assembled now?"

Think by area, not by the whole organization.

Different areas (Engineering, Product, HR, Finance, Sales) mature at different paces. Treat each as an independent track with its own target level, timeline, and priorities.

Close the loop from Day 0.

Most organizations operate in open loops: agents produce outputs, nothing returns. Every quality improvement requires manual intervention. Closed loops scale quality. The system's output produces an observable signal that returns and modifies future behavior. At N4, that signal is human judgment on each recommendation (approve/edit/reject).
