DIAGNOSIS

Where work actually changed — not where tools were adopted.

By 2026, most teams have AI tools and a few people doing impressive things with them. We look for the places where decisions are faster, handoffs are cleaner, agents can be trusted, and old rituals are starting to look unnecessary.

We examine three dimensions to uncover real change:

Culture — Who is already changing how they work, who has permission to move first, and what managers actually notice.

Technical readiness — Whether tools, docs, data, tests, and agent setup can handle real work without creating a mess.

Company system — How planning, staffing, review, metrics, and decisions change once AI is part of the daily flow.

GTM / Sales — Deal Room Agent

Walk into every deal warmer. Automates account research, call prep, follow-up, deal notes, and risk reviews. Gives managers a shared, real-time picture for coaching.

Marketing — Campaign Lab

More campaigns, more memory. Generates message variants, customer proof, landing pages, and campaign reads. Learns from performance and reuses winning patterns.

Operations — Ops Intake

Who owns this? Answered. Routes approvals, vendor requests, policy questions, and handoffs with enough context to act immediately. Reduces the time people spend as a human switchboard.

Finance — Forecast Copilot

A live pulse for planning. Pulls fresh data for forecasts, budget questions, spend variance, board prep, and scenarios.

Success / Support — Customer Pulse

See the renewal wobble sooner. Aggregates tickets, calls, product usage, renewal risk, and escalations into a brief. Allows the account team to act before the customer repeats their story.

ACCOUNTABILITY

Metrics that drive movement.

AI usage is only the starting signal. We help leaders inspect the work every week: where agents are useful, where humans are still stuck, and whether anything important is actually moving faster.

Input metrics

  • Token use — by team / role / workflow
  • Agent runs — scoped work with context and outcome
  • Review load — where humans are still the bottleneck

Output metrics

  • Adoption — who changed how they work
  • Cycle time — idea to shipped, by function
  • Decision velocity — meetings replaced by agent insight
  • Revenue per employee — the metric that separates pre-AI from post-AI
THE FRAMEWORK

AI-native in 5 levels.

Built from observing the world's most advanced AI adoptions. Each level represents a qualitatively different role for AI. The progression demands a fundamentally new direction at each jump — not just incremental improvement.

N1 — Individual productivity. People use AI to optimize personal workflows.

N2 — Team productivity. Shared agentic tools in human-designed workflows.

N3 — Contextualized OS. A single agentic layer executes work across the area.

N4 — Decision intelligence. AI proposes decisions based on human learning.

N5 — Adaptive intelligence. Autonomous learning from business outcomes.
